<h1 id="buying-and-selling-currency-pairs">Buying and selling currency pairs</h1>
<p>Currencies are traded through a forex broker or a CFD provider, and are traded in pairs.</p>
<p><code>3 categories of currency pairs:</code></p>
<ol>
<li>The “majors”</li>
<li>The “crosses”</li>
<li>The “exotics”</li>
</ol>
<h2 id="major-currency-pairs">1.) Major currency pairs</h2>
<ul>
<li>they always include the U.S. dollar</li>
<li>they are the most liquid =&gt; most frequently traded</li>
<li>the price moves more frequently which provides more trading opportunities</li>
<li><mark><strong>Liquidity</strong></mark> =&gt; describes the level of activity in the financial market
<ul>
<li>in forex, it is based on the number of active traders buying/selling a specific pair and the volume being traded</li>
<li>the more frequently something is traded, the higher its liquidity</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

</table><h2 id="exotic-currency-pairs">3.) Exotic currency pairs</h2>
<ul>
<li>they are from countries with developing or emerging economy</li>
<li>they are made up with one major currency paired with the currency of an emerging economy</li>
<li>they are not as heavily traded</li>
<li>not unusual to see spreads that are 2-3 times bigger than EUR/USD for example</li>
<li>they have lower liquidity, so they tend to be more sensitive to economic and geopolitical events</li>
</ul>

</table><h2 id="other-currency-groups">Other currency groups</h2>
<p><code>G10 currencies</code></p>
<ul>
<li>10 most heavily traded currencies in the world</li>
<li>USD, EUR, GBP, JPY, AUD, NZD, CAD, CHF, NOK, SEK, DKK</li>
</ul>
<p><code>The Scandies</code></p>
<ul>
<li>Scandinavia =&gt; Denmark, Norway, Sweden</li>
<li>together their currencies are known as “Scandies”</li>
<li>long time ago Denmark and Sweden established the “Scandinavian Monetary Union” to merge their currencies to a gold standard, Norway joined later</li>
<li>so these countries had one currency but they just minted their own coins</li>
<li>then WWI happened, the gold standard was abandoned and the Scandinavian Monetary Union split, but the countries decided to keep the currency</li>
<li>DKK, NOK, SEK</li>
</ul>
<p><code>CEE currencies</code></p>
<ul>
<li>Central and Eastern Europe</li>
<li>regarding the forex market, there are 4 currencies to be aware of</li>
<li>HUF, CZK, PLN, RON</li>
</ul>
<p><code>BRIICS</code></p>
<ul>
<li>the acronym made of 6 major emerging economies</li>
<li>BRL, RUB, INR, IDR, CNY, ZAR</li>
</ul>
<p><code>BRICS+</code></p>
<ul>
<li>Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, Saudi Arabia, UAE</li>
</ul>
