<h1 id="what-is-forex">What is Forex?</h1>
<ul>
<li><mark><strong>Forex</strong></mark> =&gt; a global financial market that allows us to trade currencies
<ul>
<li>FX/foreign exchange market</li>
<li>largest financial market in the world</li>
<li>decentralized</li>
<li>if we think one currency will be stronger vs the other one, and we are correct, then we can make a profit</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="what-is-trading">What is trading?</h2>
<p>Trading is the buying and selling of various financial assets to make a profit based on price fluctuations. We can trade virtually any financial asset that has a financial value, and us traders, we speculate on the rise/fall of that value.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><mark><strong>Exchange Rate</strong></mark> =&gt; the relative price of two currencies from two different countries</p>
<ul>
<li>it is relative, because it is not absolute or a standalone price, so it’s basically a comparative price =&gt; shows how much of one currency is needed to buy one unit of the other currency</li>
<li>e.g 1 USD = 0.95 EUR</li>
<li>this is the relative price of the euro in terms of dollars</li>
<li>the exchange rate changes by second because of the wide range of market participants (central banks, financial institutions, corporations, individual traders), so basically the market is constantly in flux (flow)</li>
<li>only a tiny percentage of currency transactions happen in the real economy involving international trade and tourism</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p><mark><strong>International Trade</strong></mark> =&gt; the exchange of goods/services between countries, also called cross-border/global trade</p>
<ul>
<li>the buying and selling of physical products (cars, clothing, electronics) and services (software, consultation, shipping)</li>
<li>settling payments for imported/exported goods/services</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Most of the currency transactions that occur in the FX market are bought/sold for speculative reasons. E.g. currency traders (currency speculators) buy currencies hoping that they will be able to sell them at a higher price in the future.</p>

</ul>
</li>
