<h1 id="forex-market-structure">Forex market structure</h1>
<h2 id="for-the-stock-market">For the stock market:</h2>
<ul>
<li>there is only one entity, one specialist that controls the prices</li>
<li>all trades must go through this specialist, because of this, prices can be easily altered to benefit the specialist and not traders</li>
<li>the specialist is forced to fulfill the orders of its clients</li>
<li>let’s say: the sellers exceeds the number of buyers</li>
<li>so in this case, the specialist is left with a bunch of stock that he cannot sell to the buyers</li>
<li>in order to prevent this from happening, the specialist will widen the spread or increase the transaction cost to prevent sellers from entering the market</li>
<li>so the specialist can manipulate the quotes to accommodate their needs</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="trading-spot-fx-is-decentralized">Trading Spot FX is decentralized</h2>
<p>In the forex market, there is no single price for a given currency at any time. We don’t have to go through a centralized exchange. The market is so huge and the competition between dealers are so big that you get the best deal almost every single time.</p>
<h2 id="the-fx-ladder">The FX ladder</h2>
<p>The forex market has a hierarchical structure that dictates how different market participants interact and trade.</p>
<p><img src="https://github.com/virag-ky/Babypips-Forex-Course-Notes/raw/main/screenshots/forex-market-structure.png" alt="enter image description here"></p>
<p>At the very top is the interbank market.<br>
The trades in the interbank market is large, involving millions/billions of dollars, contributing to the market’s overall liquidity.<br>
It consists of the largest banks in the world and they trade directly (bilaterally) with each other or through voice or electronic brokers (such as EBS Market and Reutors Matching =&gt; now called Refinitiv).<br>
The two electronic brokers quickly became the main sources of price discovery and reference prices for the entire FX spot market.<br>
All the banks in the interbank market, can see the rates that each of the electronic broker is offering, but that doesn’t mean anyone can make deals at those prices.<br>
The rates will largely depend on the established credit relationship between the trading parties.<br>
The better the credit standing and reputation with them, the better interest rates they get.</p>
<p>The hedge funds, corporations, retail market makers, and retail ECNs do not have tight credit relationships with the participants in the interbank market, so they have to do their own transactions via commercial banks =&gt; this means that their rates are slightly higher and more expensive.</p>
