<h1 id="how-to-trade-forex">How to trade forex?</h1>
<p>The most popular financial instruments are: retail forex, spot FX, currency futures, currency options, currency exchange-traded funds (ETFs), forex CFDs, and forex spread betting.</p>
<h2 id="currency-futures">Currency futures</h2>
<ul>
<li>it is a contract (agreement) to buy/sell a currency at a fixed price on a specific future date</li>
<li>they are traded on exchanges (like the Chicago Mercantile Exchange - CME)</li>
<li>very transparent and well regulated</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="currency-options">Currency options</h2>
<ul>
<li>when you buy, it gives you the right (but not the obligation) to buy/sell a currency at a fixed price by a certain date</li>
<li>when you sell an option, you maybe forced to buy/sell</li>
<li>traded on exchanges</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="currency-etfs">Currency ETFs</h2>
<ul>
<li>these are like funds, that let you invest in one currency or a group of currencies</li>
<li>you buy and sell them just like stocks on the stock market</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="spot-fx">Spot FX</h2>
<ul>
<li>the real forex market</li>
<li>traders deal with each other (mostly big banks and institutions)</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="retail-forex">Retail forex</h2>
<ul>
<li>the version of spot forex made for normal people</li>
<li>you trade through a forex broker</li>
<li>the broker acts as your counterparty</li>
<li>no one exchanges real currencies =&gt; positions are automatically “rolled over” (when you keep a trade open overnight) every day so you never have to deliver money =&gt; the broker closes your old contract and opens a new one immediately for the next day</li>
<li>when you close a trade, you just do the opposite trade to exit</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="forex-spread-betting">Forex spread betting</h2>
<ul>
<li>you bet on whether a currency pair will go up or down</li>
<li>popular in the U.K. but illegal in the U.S.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="forex-cfds">Forex CFDs</h2>
<ul>
<li>contract for difference</li>
<li>very similar to retail forex</li>
<li>you bet on the currency price to go up/down</li>
<li>the CFD provider pays you the difference if you are right, or you pay them if you are wrong</li>
<li>banned in the U.S.</li>
</ul>
