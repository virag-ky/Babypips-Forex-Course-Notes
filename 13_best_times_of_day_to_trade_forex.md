<h1 id="best-times-of-day-to-trade-forex">Best times of day to trade forex</h1>
<h2 id="tokyo-london-overlap">Tokyo-London overlap</h2>
<ul>
<li>this overlap occurs between:
<ul>
<li>Tokyo local time: 5pm-6pm</li>
<li>London local time: 8am-9am</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>the overlap lasts for about an hour, the shortest overlap</li>
<li>increased liquidity</li>
<li>potential for volatility</li>
<li>lower trading volumes compared to London-New York overlap</li>
<li>Asian traders closing their positions, European traders open their positions</li>
<li>important economic data from Asia and Europe may be released during this period</li>
<li>some traders focus on breakout strategies during this period as prices might start trending as European traders enter the market</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="london-new-york-overlap">London-New York overlap</h2>
<ul>
<li>this overlap occurs between:
<ul>
<li>London local time: 1pm-5pm</li>
<li>New York local time: 8am-12pm</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>longest overlap, about 4 hours</li>
<li>highest liquidity and trading volume</li>
<li>increased volatility</li>
<li>tighter spreads</li>
<li>during this period where the WMR Spot Benchmark Rate is determined</li>
<li>the rate is set at 4pm London time, known as “London fix”</li>
<li><mark><strong>Currency fixing</strong></mark> =&gt; a set time each day when the prices of currencies for commercial transactions are set or fixed</li>
<li>since currency prices fluctuates every second, a daily “reference point” is needed</li>
</ul>
