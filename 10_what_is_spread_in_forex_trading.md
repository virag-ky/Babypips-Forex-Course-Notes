<h1 id="what-is-spread-in-forex-trading">What is spread in forex trading?</h1>
<ul>
<li><mark><strong>Spread</strong></mark> =&gt; difference between bid/ask price</li>
</ul>
<p>The spread is how no commission brokers make their money. This “spread” is the fee for providing transaction immediacy. Instead of charging a separate fee for making a trade, the cost is built into the buy/sell price. The broker provides a service and needs to make money somehow:</p>
<ul>
<li>they make money by selling the currency to you for more than they paid for it</li>
<li>they also make money by buying the currency from you for less than they will receive when they sell it</li>
<li>this difference is the spread</li>
</ul>
<p>When the broker says “no commission” or “zero commission”, you are still paying the built-in spread.</p>
<h2 id="how-is-the-spread-measured">How is the spread measured?</h2>
<p>The spread is usually measured in pips.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>e.g. EUR/USD</p>
<ul>
<li>EUR -&gt; 1.1051</li>
<li>USD -&gt; 1.1053</li>
<li>spread = 1.1053 - 1.1051 (ask - bid) =&gt; so the spread is 2 pips</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>e.g. USD/JPY</p>
<ul>
<li>USD -&gt; 110.00</li>
<li>JPY -&gt; 110.04</li>
<li>spread = 110.04 - 110.00 =&gt; 4 pips</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="what-types-of-spreads-are-in-forex">What types of spreads are in forex?</h2>
<p>The type of spread that you’ll see on a trading platform depends on the forex broker and how they make money.</p>
<p>1.) fixed spreads<br>
2.) variable spreads (floating)</p>
<h3 id="fixed-spreads">1.) Fixed spreads</h3>
<p>Fixed spreads are usually offered by brokers that operate as a market maker or “dealing desk” model.<br>
Fixed spreads stay the same regardless of what market conditions are at any given time.<br>
Using a dealing desk, the broker buys large positions from their liquidity providers and offers these positions in smaller sizes to traders. This means that the broker acts as the counterparty to their customers’ trades. Having a dealing desk, allows the broker to offer fixed spreads because they can control the prices they display to their customers.</p>
<h4 id="advantages">Advantages:</h4>
<ul>
<li>fixed spreads have a smaller capital requirement, so it offers cheaper alternative for traders who don’t have lot of money to start trading with</li>
<li>makes calculating transaction costs more predictable</li>
<li>fixed spreads never change, you are always sure of what you can expect to pay when you open a trade</li>
</ul>
<h4 id="disadvantages">Disadvantages:</h4>
<ul>
<li>requotes can occur frequently since pricing is coming from just one source (the broker)</li>
<li>slippage is another problem =&gt; when prices are moving fast, the broker is unable to consistently maintain a fixed spread and the price that you finally end up with after entering the trade will be totally different than the intended entry price</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="variable-spreads">2.) Variable spreads</h3>
<p>They are always changing.<br>
They are offered by non-dealing desk brokers who get their pricing from multiple liquidity providers and pass on these prices to the trader without the intervention of a dealing desk. So they have no control over spreads.<br>
Spreads will tighten and widen based on the supply and demand of currencies and the overall market volatility.<br>
Typically spreads widen during economic data releases as well as other periods when the liquidity in the market decreases (like on holidays)</p>
<ul>
<li>e.g. normal spread =&gt; 2 pips, wide spread =&gt; 20 pips</li>
</ul>
<h4 id="advantages-1">Advantages:</h4>
<ul>
<li>they eliminate experiencing requotes</li>
<li>provides more transparent pricing =&gt; meaning, having access from multiple liquidity providers usually means better pricing due to competition</li>
</ul>
<h4 id="disadvantages-1">Disadvantages:</h4>
<ul>
<li>not ideal for scalpers, the widened spreads can quickly eat into any profits that the scalper makes</li>
<li>not ideal for news traders, spread may widen so much that what looks like a profitable one can turn into an unprofitable one very quickly</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="spread-costs-and-calculations">Spread costs and calculations</h2>
<ul>
<li>e.g. EUR/USD
<ul>
<li>EUR -&gt; 1.35626</li>
<li>USD -&gt; 1.35640</li>
<li>spread =&gt; 1.4 pips</li>
<li>1.4 pips x 1 mini lot (10,000 units) x $1 (pip cost per mini lot) =&gt; $1.40 spread cost</li>
<li>for 5 mini lots =&gt; 5 x 1.40</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
